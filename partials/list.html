Lost NYC Music Landmarks

An Ever-Changing City

  • Music venues come and go, especially in New York City. In some eras, jazz clubs lined entire city blocks, notably 52nd Street, which hosted a row of notable after-hours joints, including Birdland, during the '30s and '40s. In the 1960s and '70s, rock clubs fourished on the Lower East Side, while in the present day, Brooklyn has become a hotbed of experimental sounds. Classical music venues have seen turnover, too, notably with the shuttering of the Old Met in 1967. Where else can the awning of punk club CBGB's sell for $30,000 - reportedly 500 times the original monthly rent. With that in mind, we present this brief guide to the famous halls that have come and gone over the decades in New York.
